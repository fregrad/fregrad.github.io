FreGrad: lightweight and fast frequency-aware diffusion vocoder

Tan Dat Nguyen, Ji-Hoon Kim, Youngjoon Jang, Jaehun Kim, Joon Son Chung
Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology, South Korea

FreGrad gradually remove noise from wavelet features

Abstract

The goal of this paper is to generate realistic audio with a lightweight and fast diffusion-based vocoder, named FreGrad. Our framework consists of the following three key components: (1) We employ discrete wavelet transform to decompose complicated waveform into sub-band audios, which helps FreGrad to operate on simple and concise feature space, (2) We design a frequency-aware dilated convolution that elevates frequency awareness, resulting in enhanced frequency-consistency between generated and target audio, and (3) We introduce a bag of tricks that boosts the generation performance of the proposed model. In our experiments, FreGrad achieves 4 times faster training time and 2.2 times faster inference speed compared to baseline while reducing the model size to 1.5 times (only 1.78M parameters) without sacrificing the output quality.
